<section class="shell page-intro narrow">
  <p class="eyebrow">Responsible use</p>
  <h1>Learn, build and test responsibly.</h1>
  <p>I share cybersecurity projects and notes to help people learn, understand systems and improve security—not to enable harm.</p>
</section>
<section class="shell content-split responsible-content">
  <div class="prose">
    <h2>Keep it authorised</h2>
    <p>Only test systems, networks and accounts that you own or have clear permission to assess. Laws, policies and acceptable-use rules still apply when a technique is published for educational purposes.</p>
    <h2>Use your judgement</h2>
    <p>I do my best to share accurate information, but technology changes and mistakes happen. Check commands before running them, understand the impact and use an isolated environment when appropriate.</p>
    <h2>Make things safer</h2>
    <p>If you discover a vulnerability, follow responsible disclosure practices and give the affected party a reasonable opportunity to fix it before sharing details publicly.</p>
  </div>
  <aside class="principles-card">
    <p class="eyebrow">The short version</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Get permission first</li>
      <li>Understand before running</li>
      <li>Protect other people's data</li>
      <li>Share findings responsibly</li>
    </ul>
  </aside>
</section>
